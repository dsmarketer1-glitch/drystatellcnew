Signs of Water Damage

Early detection can save thousands in restoration costs. Contact us immediately if you notice any of these red flags.

water_drop

Discoloration

Yellow, brown, or copper-colored stains appearing on ceilings or walls.

air_freshener

Musty Odors

Persistent damp smells that don't go away with cleaning or ventilation.

format_paint

Bubbling Walls

Peeling paint or wallpaper and drywall that feels soft or spongy to the touch.

Common Sources of Damage

plumbing

Plumbing Failures

Burst pipes, leaking water heaters, and overflowing toilets are the most frequent causes of indoor flooding.

home_repair_service

Roof & Foundation Leaks

Aging shingles or cracked foundations allow storm water to seep into living spaces during heavy rain.

kitchen

Appliance Malfunctions

Washing machine hoses and refrigerator supply lines can fail suddenly, releasing hundreds of gallons of water.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the restoration process.

How long does the drying process take? expand_more
Most water-damaged properties can be fully dried within 3 to 5 days. However, the exact duration depends on the volume of water, the types of materials affected (e.g., hardwood vs. carpet), and how quickly we are able to begin the extraction process.
Will my insurance cover this? expand_more
In most cases, sudden and accidental water damage (like burst pipes or appliance overflows) is covered by standard homeowner's insurance. We work directly with your insurance company to help document the damage and assist with the claim process.
Is the noise from the fans safe? expand_more
Yes, the noise from industrial air movers is safe, though it can be loud (similar to a heavy-duty household vacuum). While it may be inconvenient, it is critical that these fans remain running 24/7 during the drying phase to prevent mold growth and secondary damage.
